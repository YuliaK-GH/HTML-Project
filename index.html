Bugs are everywhere...

Software bugs are an unavoidable part of development, often arising from the complexity of modern systems, human error, incomplete testing, or environmental differences. While some bugs may seem minor, their consequences can be significant, especially when discovered in production.


Why Bugs Are Everywhere?

  • Complex Software: The complexity and interdependencies in modern software make it difficult to catch every bug.
  • Human Error: Developers, despite their expertise, can overlook mistakes.
  • Testing Limitations: Not all scenarios can be tested before release.
  • Environmental Factors: Software may behave differently in real-world conditions.
